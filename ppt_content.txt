## Slide 2: 1. Problem Statement
- **Manual Dependency:** Reliance on physical registers leads to high error rates and labor-intensive tracking.
- **Stock Wastage:** Lack of automated expiry monitoring results in significant financial losses due to expired medicines.
- **Data Entry Errors:** Inaccurate records of batch numbers and MRPs cause billing discrepancies.
- **Return Deadlines:** Pharmacists often miss distributor return windows due to lack of pre-expiry alerts.
- **Stock Optimization:** No data-driven way to identify dead stock or fast-moving items.

---

## Slide 3: 2. Literature Survey
- **Inventory Theory:** Efficient stock handling is critical for time-sensitive pharmaceutical products (Axsäter, 2005).
- **Inaccuracy Impact:** Record inaccuracy in retail pharmacy leads to customer dissatisfaction and stockouts (DeHoratius, 2008).
- **Automation Benefits:** Barcode and OCR technologies are proven to reduce human error in medicinal data entry.
- **Relational Databases:** SQL-based systems ensure data integrity and quick retrieval for complex batch-medicine relationships.

---

## Slide 4: 3. Research Gap
- **Cost vs. Complexity:** Existing enterprise solutions are either too expensive for local pharmacies or too complex to operate without IT staff.
- **Niche Focus:** Most generic POS systems lack specific pharmaceutical features like 'Batch-wise FIFO' or 'Ingredient-based Matching'.
- **Return Management:** Significant lack of automated "Return-Eligibility" flagging in small-scale systems.
- **Hybrid Entry:** Most systems force either 100% Barcode or 100% Manual; PharmaSync fills this gap with a hybrid OCR/Barcode/Manual approach.

---

## Slide 5: 4. Objective of Research
1. **Batch Tracking:** Implement granular monitoring of Mfg/Exp dates at the batch level.
2. **Flexible Entry:** Design a hybrid mechanism (OCR, Barcode, Auto-suggest) for stock entry.
3. **Automated Alerts:** Create a multi-tier notification system for near-expiry products.
4. **FIFO Sales:** Integrate a First-In-First-Out stock deduction logic for sales.
5. **Return Efficiency:** Automate the identification of return-eligible batches.
6. **Analytics:** Provide a visual dashboard for data-driven decision making.

---

## Slide 6: 5. Methodology (Objective Wise)
- **Obj 1 (Batching):** Relational Schema Design linking `Medicine` to `Batch` with unique expiry constraints.
- **Obj 2 (Hybrid Entry):** Integration of OCR libraries and Barcode logic with a fallback auto-suggestion engine.
- **Obj 3 (Alerts):** Date-difference algorithm categorized by threshold (30/90 days) with color-coded UI indicators.
- **Obj 4 (FIFO):** Backend logic that sorts batches by `expiry_date` (ASC) during checkout to ensure oldest stock sells first.
- **Obj 5 (Returns):** Algorithm-based flagging of batches within the 90-180 day pre-expiry window.
- **Obj 6 (Analytics):** Real-time data aggregation using Django ORM and visualization with Recharts.

---

## Slide 7: 6. Result Discussion
- **Zero Inaccuracy:** Successfully prevented negative stock and duplicate batch-mismatch errors.
- **Waste Reduction:** Automated FIFO logic significantly minimizes the risk of stock expiring on shelves.
- **Operational Speed:** Billing time reduced through smart search and automatic stock deduction.
- **User Interface:** Implemented a responsive, dark-mode-enabled UI that ensures usability in low-light pharmacy environments.
- **Data Export:** Functional CSV reporting allows pharmacists to perform offline audits and detailed analysis.
